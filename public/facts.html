RICE FACTS & HEALTH


Rice is an annual grain that is harvested once a year. The cultivation of rice is suited for countries with low labor costs and high rainfall as it is very labor intensive and requires large amounts of water for cultivation. Small rice seedlings are hand planted into rice paddies that are then filled with water. The seeds draw nutrients from the paddies water, which is also the same water.



There are more than 40,000 different varieties of rice. Of the 40,000 varieties, more than 100 are grown worldwide, but only around 10 percent are marketed and sold.


WORLD RELATED RICE FACTS


=> Farming of Rice is traced back to around 5,000 BC.


=> Almost 6.6 billion which makes nearly half of the world eat rice as their staple food.


=> By 2030 the demand for rice is expected to grow by 50%.


=> The cultivation of rice takes place in more than 100 countries and every continent except Antarctica.


=> More than 40,000 different varieties of rice is present in the world.


=> 65 kilos of rice are milled annually for every person on earth.


=> Almost 96% of the world’s rice is consumed in the area in which it is grown.




RICE NUTRITIONAL FACTS


=> Rice is cholesterol free and is low in fats.


=>Rice is very rich in carbohydrates; approximately a rice kernel contains 85% of carbohydrates.


=> Rice contains less than 5mg sodium per 100gm serve and is best suited for people who want to watch their salt intake.


=> Rice when cooked swells to give at least three times its original weight
